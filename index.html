The amphetamine epidemic:
ADHD prescribing in England, 2015-2025

The number of children and adults receiving medication for ADHD has risen dramatically over the past decade. NHS data reveal who is being prescribed stimulants, where, and how the picture is changing.

Adam Chidlow  ·  Data: NHS Business Services Authority; NHS England Digital Data; Office for National Statistics  · 

Amphetamine-class drugs are now mainstream NHS medication

Monthly items dispensed by drug type. Methylphenidate has long dominated, but lisdexamfetamine, a direct amphetamine derivative, has grown faster than any other ADHD drug since its UK approval. Total stimulant prescriptions now exceed 300,000 items per month.

Source: NHSBSA Medicines Used in Mental Health, monthly chemical substance data

For the first time, more adults than children are being prescribed ADHD drugs

Identified patients receiving CNS stimulants in England by financial year. Adults first outnumbered children in 2022/23. The gap has widened every year since.

Source: NHSBSA Medicines Used in Mental Health, 2015/16–2024/25

Covid hit children's prescriptions hard, while adults' barely paused

Growth rate of stimulant prescriptions for ADHD by age group (identified patients only, ages 17 and under; 18 and over). Children's prescribing has never fully recovered its pre-pandemic trajectory while adult prescribing still sustains 25% year-on-year growth.

Source: NHSBSA Medicines Used in Mental Health, 2015/16–2024/25

Women in their thirties are the fastest-growing age group, but teenage boys remain the largest

Male patients aged 10–14 remain the single largest group by far, but the fastest growth is among women aged 30–34. Toggle between five-year growth and 2025 patient numbers.

Prescribing rates vary dramatically by region

Some areas have seen ADHD prescribing more than triple since 2015/16 while others have grown far more slowly. Toggle between ten-year growth rates for the top and bottom five ICBs, and population-standardised prescription totals for 2024/5. Hover to see both.

Source: NHSBSA Medicines Used in Mental Health; ONS Mid-year Population Estimates

Where therapy is hardest to access, ADHD prescribing has grown fastest

Each spot is one of England's 42 Integrated Care Boards, plotted against their NHS Talking Therapies six-week access rate and five-year growth in ADHD prescribing. Red spots are the top and bottom five ICBs by five-year prescription growth. Spot size reflects talking therapies throughput per 100,000. Trend line: r = -0.34.
